## Pierrot Route Guide

This page is for players who already know they want to follow Pierrot more closely and need a cleaner way to think about save points, emotional patterns, and route pressure.

## What defines the Pierrot route

- Emotional dependency and unstable tenderness matter more here than blunt strategy.
- Scenes that look soft or intimate often carry more route weight than they first appear to.
- The route works best when you pay attention to reassurance, pity, trust, and how vulnerability is handled.

## When to make saves

- Save before emotionally loaded one-on-one scenes.
- Save before moments where care, suspicion, or withdrawal feel like the real choice beneath the dialogue.
- Keep at least one Pierrot-focused master slot separate from balanced or lore-first runs.

## Spoiler-light route advice

- Do not assume the safest-sounding choice is always the least consequential one.
- Track whether you are reinforcing closeness or undermining it over time.
- If a scene feels like a fragile emotional offer, treat it as a possible route pivot.
